ORIGIN

Early Accounts of Turumba

The Turumba tradition traces its roots to the town of Pakil, Laguna, where faith and devotion to Our Lady of Sorrows gave birth to a celebration unlike any other.

The word ‘Turumba’ is believed to have gone through various interpretations over time. Today, it is defined as a state of losing oneself or pagkawala sa sarili — a meaning recorded in books commonly available at the church of Pakil. This interpretation reflects how the people experience Turumba.
